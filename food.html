National Cuisine

Sour cabbage

In world more famous as Sauerkraut, it is raw cabbage that has been fermented by various lactic acid bacteria. It has a long shelf life and a distinctive sour flavor, and it is used as salad (cut and served with oil and paprika) or used for many dishes where it has to be cooked or roasted.

Sarma

This delicius meal is a rolled sour cabbage filled with minced mean and rice. It is one of the dishes students miss the most when they go away to a bigger city for their studies. If you do not see sarma on a non-fasting slava, then the other guests have probably already eaten it!

Ćevapi

These short little peaces of minced meat are a very popular choice in restaurants, as much as on fast food kiosks. They are often served with onion. Choose wisely in which city you want to try ćevapi!

Pasulj

These are not Mexican style beans, it's a dish for itself and there are different types of it, depending on the way of preparing and other ingredients put with it.

Stuffed Peppers

As their name says (in Serbian ''punjene paprike''), bigger peppers are filled with minced meat and rice and are closed on the top with a potato or a tomato slice.

Turšija

In Serbia housewives make ''zimnica'' — winter supplies of vegetables preserved and prepared in a special way. Sour cabbage is also part of zimnica, together with turšija. They take pickled cucumbers, cauliflowers, tomatos, carrots and peppers and put them in jars so they can be eaten during winter before vegetables start growing again.

Ajvar

One more part of zimnica made of peppers. It is one of the cuisine brands Serbians are most proud of. Peppers are cooked in a big pot making a mixture they put in jars.

Gibanica

It is a type of a pie — a traditional pastry dish popular all over the Balkans. It is usually made with cottage cheese and eggs.

Rakija

Favorite drink in Serbia! Original one, šljivovica, is a brandy made of plums, while there can be rakija made of all kinds of fruit. Make sure you gather courage and try this one when visiting Serbia!
